Admission Requirements

Ensure you meet the eligibility criteria for your chosen program before applying.

B.Tech (Biomedical Engineering)

  • Passed 10+2 examination with Physics/Mathematics/Chemistry/Computer Science/Electronics/Information Technology/Biology/Informatics Practices/Biotechnology/Technical Vocational subject with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

B.Tech (Biotechnology)

  • Passed 10+2 examination with Physics/Mathematics/Chemistry/Computer Science/Electronics/Information Technology/Biology/Informatics Practices/Biotechnology/Technical Vocational subject with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

B.Tech (All Branches)

  • Passed 10+2 examination with Physics/Mathematics/Chemistry/Computer Science/Electronics/Information Technology/Biology/Informatics Practices/Biotechnology/Technical Vocational subject/Agriculture/Engineering Graphics/Business Studies/Entrepreneurship with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category). Refer Table 8.4
    OR
    Passed minimum 3-year Diploma examination with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category), subject to vacancies in the First Year for lateral entry. (Bridge courses like Mathematics, Physics, Engineering Drawing, etc., will be offered for diverse backgrounds.)

B.Tech (Lateral Entry)

  • Passed minimum 3-year/2-year (Lateral Entry) Diploma examination with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category) in any branch of Engineering and Technology.
    OR
    Passed B.Sc. Degree from a recognized university (as defined by UGC) with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category) and 10+2 with Mathematics.
    OR
    Passed D.Voc. Stream in the same or allied sector. Refer Table 1.10 of Appendix-1 (Bridge courses will be offered.)

M.Tech (Civil Engineering - Construction Management / Environmental Engineering / Structural Engineering / Transportation Engineering & Planning)

  • Passed Bachelor’s Degree or equivalent in the relevant field with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).

M.Tech (Valuation - Civil, Land & Building)

  • Passed B.E./B.Tech. Civil or B.Arch with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).

Master of Planning (Town and Country Planning)

  • Passed B.Arch, B.Tech/BE (Civil), Postgraduate (Geography/Sociology/Economics), or B.Planning with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).

M.Tech (Electrical Vehicles)

  • Passed Bachelor’s Degree or equivalent in Automotive, Electrical, Electrical and Electronics, Mechanical, Electronics and Telecommunication, Electronics and Communication, Instrumentation, or Electronics with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).

B.Sc. (Computer Science)

  • Passed 10+2 Science with Mathematics from a recognized board with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

BCA

  • Passed 10+2 or equivalent with 45% marks (40% for reserved category) and English as a subject.
    OR
    Passed 3-year Diploma (after SSC) or 2-year Diploma in Pharmacy (after HSC) or 10+2 with any vocational subject with 45% marks.

MCA

  • Passed BCA/B.Sc. (Computer Science/IT)/B.E./B.Tech. (CSE/IT) or equivalent, or any graduation degree (e.g., B.E./B.Tech./B.Sc./B.Com./B.A./B.Voc.) with Mathematics at 10+2 or graduation level, with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category). (Bridge courses in Mathematics and computer subjects may be required.)

B.Tech (Computer Science & Engineering - Cloud Technology and Information Security / Cyber Security and Forensic / Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning)

  • Passed 10+2 examination with Physics/Mathematics/Chemistry/Computer Science/Electronics/Information Technology/Biology/Informatics Practices/Biotechnology/Technical Vocational subject/Agriculture/Engineering Graphics/Business Studies/Entrepreneurship with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category). Refer Table 8.4
    OR
    Passed minimum 3-year Diploma examination with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category). (Bridge courses will be offered.)

B.Tech (Computer Science & Engineering - Lateral Entry)

  • Passed minimum 3-year/2-year (Lateral Entry) Diploma examination with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category) in any branch of Engineering and Technology.
    OR
    Passed B.Sc. Degree from a recognized university (as defined by UGC) with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category) and 10+2 with Mathematics.
    OR
    Passed D.Voc. Stream in the same or allied sector. Refer Table 1.10 of Appendix-1 (Bridge courses will be offered.)

M.Tech (Computer Science & Engineering - Cloud Technology and Information Security)

  • Passed Bachelor’s Degree or equivalent in the relevant field with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).

BBA (Digital Marketing)

  • Passed 10+2 or equivalent in any discipline from a recognized board with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

MBA

  • Minimum 55% in any undergraduate degree or valid score in CAT/XAT/MAT, any state CET, or Brahma Valley University Joint Entrance Exam (BVJEE - MBA).

B.Com (with Tally Certification)

  • Passed 10+2 or equivalent in any discipline from a recognized board with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

D.Pharm

  • Passed 10+2 examination with Physics and Chemistry as compulsory subjects along with Mathematics or Biology.

B.Pharm

  • Passed 10+2 examination with English, Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics or Biology as optional subjects individually.

M.Pharm

  • Passed B.Pharm from an institution approved by the Pharmacy Council of India with at least 55% marks (aggregate of 4 years).

B.A.LLB / B.A.LLB (Hons)

  • Passed 10+2 or equivalent in any discipline from a recognized board with at least 45% marks (42% for OBC, 40% for SC/ST).

LLB (Hons)

  • Passed Bachelor’s Degree (minimum 3 years) in any discipline from a recognized university with at least 45% marks (42% for OBC, 40% for SC/ST).

LL.M (Criminal Law / Constitutional & Administrative Law / Corporate & Commercial Law)

  • Passed Bachelor’s Degree in Law from a recognized institute under BCI rules with at least 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/OBC). Must appear in BVU PG, PG CLAT, or PG SLAT.

B.Sc. (Microbiology / Forensic Science)

  • Passed 10+2 Science or equivalent from a recognized board with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category) or a 3-year Diploma from a recognized Board of Technical Education.

B.Sc. (Chemistry)

  • Passed 10+2 with Chemistry as a compulsory subject with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

B.Sc. (Physics)

  • Passed 10+2 with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

B.Sc. (Computational Mathematics and Data Science)

  • Passed 10+2 with Mathematics as a compulsory subject with at least 45% marks (40% for reserved category).

M.Sc. (Chemistry / Microbiology / Life Science)

  • Graduation in the respective stream or equivalent with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).

M.Sc. (Physics)

  • Passed B.Sc. (any branch) or B.E./B.Tech. with Physics and Mathematics courses with at least 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/OBC).

M.Sc. (Mathematics)

  • Passed B.Sc. Mathematics, General B.Sc. with Mathematics, or B.E./B.Tech. with at least 50% marks (45% for SC/ST/OBC).

M.Sc. (Forensic Science)

  • Graduation in Forensic Science or B.Sc. in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Botany, Zoology, Biotechnology, Microbiology, Biochemistry, Bioinformatics, Biophysics, Anthropology, Medicine, or B.Pharm with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).

B.Sc. (Interior Design & Decoration / Beauty Cosmetology / Fashion & Apparel Design)

  • Passed 10+2 or equivalent in any discipline from a recognized board with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).
    OR
    Passed 10th Diploma in any stream (or Pharmacy for Beauty Cosmetology).

B.Des (Communication Design / Product Design / Space & Interior Design / Fashion & Lifestyle Design)

  • Passed 10+2 or equivalent in any discipline from a recognized board with at least 50% marks (40% for reserved category).
    OR
    Passed 10th Diploma in any stream.

M.Sc. (Beauty Cosmetology / Fashion Design)

  • Graduation in any stream or equivalent professional course from a recognized university with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved category).
